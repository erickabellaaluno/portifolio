Objective
==============
Explore the use of LLMs to support the formulation of computational problems while developing independent logical reasoning and algorithmic problem-solving skills.

Activity Description
==============
Each student must propose two problems involving vectors/lists with the help of an LLM (for example, ChatGPT). The problems should be clearly related to the student's degree area, such as:
Mechanical Engineering
Electrical Engineering
Telecommunications Engineering
The problems should reflect realistic or plausible situations within these areas.

Requirements
==============

Problem formulation
Use an LLM to help define the two problems.
The problems must involve operations with vectors/lists (e.g., search, sum, average, filtering, comparison, etc.).
They must be contextualized in the student's course area.

Problem solving
Solve the two problems manually, using:
pen and paper
your own language (algorithmic description, step-by-step)
It is not allowed to use LLM-generated code or programming tools in this stage.

Deliverable
==============
An image (photo or scan) containing:
the two defined problems
the respective handwritten solutions

Evaluation Criteria
============================
Clarity and relevance of the proposed problems
Suitability to the use of vectors/lists
Correctness and coherence of the solutions
Quality of the algorithmic explanation (organization, logic, clarity)

Important notes
============================
Use of LLMs is allowed only in the problem-creation stage.
The solutions must reflect the student's own understanding, without dependence on automated tools.
Generic or disconnected problems relative to the student's area may receive reduced scores.
